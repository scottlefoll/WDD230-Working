Fish Haven on Bear Lake

In southeastern Idaho a tiny town perches on the banks of beautiful Bear Lake. The lakes lovely shores have been a place of respite and sustenance for generations, and today the lake and the town draw visitors from around the area and around the world. The town is certainly small - it has only a couple of hundred full-time residents.

The town is a small, but lively, community. It is a place where people can come to relax, make friends and enjoy the outdoors. And, of course, fish. As in "Fish Haven."

There are records of early fishing in the lake going back to pioneer days and even back among the native americans. The lake is a popular destination for fishing and boating, and the town draws a large crows each year for the annual fishing festival. But if you want to come, make sure you have a permit, and an electric motor on your boat, and you'll be sure to enjoy the fishing. The rangers are strict about the rules, but they are generous with the permits. You can get a permit at Mel's Outdoor Sports on Barnes Road.

If you'd like more information go to: BearLake.org
