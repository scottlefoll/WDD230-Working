My code journal

Purpose

I am in the middle of a pretty dramatic career change. I’ve been an organic farmer for the last 12 years, growing healthy vegetables. It’s been a blast, and very rewarding, but it’s hard to bring up 5 kids on it financially. So, while we will keep farming, I’m leaving the vegetables to the rest of the family this year and working on another love of mine, something that I have always wanted to do - become a software engineer.

Even worse, I’m trying to do it in my fifties, at an age far older than the usual beginning programmer. Yeah, I know, it's a bit nuts.

This is a study blog about my journey to become a professional software developer. I’ll put down my successes and failures, what worked and what didn’t. I’ll document all my failed interviews and applications, even my experiences with the FANG guys, to check out how really diverse they can be. (yeah, umm, does diversity really extend to old guys, lol?)

It’s an exercise in self-reflection, a journal of the journey, a roadmap for myself and maybe others, and a means for me to assess my progress. And there is a sliver of a chance that it might get read by a potential employer someday.

Currently, I am enrolled as a computer science student at BYU Idaho. In addition, I am working my way through “100 Days of Code” on Udemy and also the “Complete 2022 Web Dev Bootcamp on Udemy, both well-regarded courses by Angela Yu that are exact online versions of two 12 week bootcamps she runs at The London App Brewery; the Deep Learning Nanodegree course on Udacity; I’m self-studying for the Comptia A+ exam in IT Support. I have some additional Python learning resources (Free Code Camp, The Odin Project, and Codecademy) that I use regularly.

Basically, I am in full-time, all-in, professional student mode. I spend all my time either doing class work for the university, working on my other certifications, watching videos about coding or reading coding books. I think it would be interesting and helpful to me to write about what I am doing, and it might be interesting and helpful to others.

Almost daily I find very cool and really useful tidbits or nuggets, which I notebook and save for myself. So I think others who are in the earlier phases of their coding adventure might benefit from reading about those.

They could be as minor as a cool theme for VS Code or PyCharm, or as huge as the new PyScript front-end library.

Introduction

My name is Scott and I’m trying to change my life. I’m in my fifties, and I’m trying to switch from being an organic farmer to a professional software developer. Now before it sounds too crazy, I did coding work for about 4 years, but it was 20 years ago. It was all self-taught and only in something called Visual Basic.

So I’m spending this entire year “retooling” (I think that’s a euphemism for swapping out gears and refurbishing the brain, just like you might do to a used computer).

I have a plan of “attack” - yeah, I’m a older American guy, so everything is in terms of conflict. I have lots and lots of people I follow on Twitter, channels I subscribe to on Youtube, blog feeds I get, and courses I am enrolled in. Probably too many, but I’m sure I’m overcompensating for my age handicap.

Oh, I’m also enrolled full time as a Computer Science major at Brigham Young University - Idaho. That’s what actually takes the majority of my time right now.

That’s it. This site is the journal of that journey. One year from knowing very, very little to becoming as proficient as I can become.

PyScript Released

Big news last week with the public release of PyScript, the python library that will allow python to easily handle and integrate front-end tasks of great complexity. This gives python developers quick access to all the libraries and capabilities of python from within the browser on the front-end.

Here is an article on pyscript from the people at Anaconda: PyScript: Python in the Browser

Python's greatest strength is is extensibility. With so many incredibly bright scientists and mathmaticians using python for such a wide diversity of complex tasks, they end up creating libraries that then become publicly available.

Python Speed Boost

Python is getting a big speed boost. In another big announcement, the release of CPython was delivered in San Francisco. A response to the 2021 priority goal from Guido van Rossum of boosting python's speed by up to 200% jumped the development of CPython. Written in C, it gives direct api access to foundational C program calls.

"I want Python to be more effective for large projects, without losing sight of its use for small projects and teaching," says Python creator Guido van Rossum.

Swap Two Variables

My first tip is very simple, but very useful. Use this snippet to swap the values of 2 variables with the least code:

var x = 6;
var y = 7;
[x,y] = [y,x]
console.log(x,y) // 7 6

Sort a String Array Alphabetically

Use this little bit of code to quickly and easily sort a string array:

function alphabetSort(arr)
{
return arr.sort((a, b) = a.localeCompare(b))
}
let array = ["d", "c", "b", "a"]
